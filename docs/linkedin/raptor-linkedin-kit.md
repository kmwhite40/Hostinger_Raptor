## 5. First-6-weeks content plan

**Three content pillars** — rotate through them so the feed stays balanced:

1. **Authority** — CMMC/NIST/Zero Trust explainers, deadlines, "what this control actually means." Builds trust with GovCon buyers.
2. **Capability** — What Raptor does: Shield/Overwatch/Pathfinder, Microsoft Government cloud, SOC operations. Positions you against competitors.
3. **Identity** — Veteran-led, SDVOSB, values (Integrity / Excellence / Commitment), team, milestones. Makes the brand human.

**Cadence — 2 posts per week is sustainable for a small team:**

| Week | Post A (Tue) | Post B (Thu) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Launch announcement *(Post 1)* | Capabilities *(Post 2)* |
| 2 | CMMC hook *(Post 3)* | Identity: "Why veteran-led matters in federal IT" |
| 3 | Authority: NIST 800-171 in plain English | Capability: What GCC High is and who needs it |
| 4 | Authority: Top 3 CMMC readiness mistakes | Identity: Our core values, and why they're non-negotiable |
| 5 | Capability: XDR vs. legacy antivirus | Authority: What "Zero Trust" actually requires |
| 6 | Identity/proof: a milestone, cert, or lesson learned | Authority: The real cost of a CUI breach |

**Operating tips**
- Post between 8–10am CT on weekdays; GovCon audiences are on early.
- Always end with either a question (drives comments) or a single link to raptor-ent.com — not both.
- Have the founder repost each company post from their personal profile. On LinkedIn, personal reach beats page reach 5–10×.
- Use 3–5 hashtags, not 15. Keep #SDVOSB, #CMMC, and #GovCon as your anchor tags.
